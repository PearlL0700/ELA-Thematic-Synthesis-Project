Introduction

Fear, an instinct ingrained in the human psyche, often influences individuals to resort to violence as a coping mechanism, allowing them to assert dominance and maintain control in threatening situations. It manifests itself as the quickening of the pulse, the sweat on the brow, and the adrenaline surge that propels individuals into action, giving them motivation to defend themselves or lash out against perceived threats. Yet fear simultaneously catalyzes personal growth, urging one to confront and face their challenges head-on. From psychological studies to literary analyses, by analyzing humans' response to fear, a pattern emerges regarding human nature: humans can respond to fear in multiple ways. Reacting to it with destructive behavior, such as violence, or constructive outcomes, such as personal development and resilience. This duality highlights the complexity of fear as a driving force in human behavior. Understanding this pattern allows one to navigate challenges posed by fear more effectively, shedding light on the nuanced nature of fear and its profound impact on the human experience.
